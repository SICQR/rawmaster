How it works

The pipeline,
step by step.

01

Load + Resample

Audio loaded at 44100 Hz, converted to float32. Supports MP3, WAV, FLAC, AIFF, OGG, M4A.

02

Spectral Gating

Non-stationary noise reduction via noisereduce. prop_decrease=0.6 — aggressive enough to kill shimmer and hiss, safe enough to preserve transients and low-end.

noisereduce
03

LUFS Normalisation

Integrated loudness measured and normalised to −14.0 LUFS. The streaming standard: Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube.

pyloudnorm
04

Hard Limiter

Peak ceiling at −0.3 dBFS. Clean, ISP-safe output with no inter-sample distortion.

04b

Reference Mastering (optional)

When a reference track is provided via --ref, spectral profile, loudness, and dynamics are matched to the reference. Replaces the fixed LUFS + limiter chain.

matchering
05

Stem Separation

HTDemucs fine-tuned model with 2 shifts and 0.25 overlap. Best-in-class open-source separation. Runs on CPU or GPU.

htdemucs_ft
06

MIDI Extraction

Spotify's Basic-Pitch model converts bass stem to polyphonic MIDI. Note activations at frame level — accurate, expressive output.

basic-pitch

FAQ

Questions.
Answered.

How long does processing take?

BPM + key detection: ~5 seconds. Remastering: ~10 seconds. Stem separation: 5-15 minutes on CPU, 1-3 minutes with a GPU. MIDI extraction: ~10 seconds. Total for a full run on CPU: around 10-20 minutes per track.

What are the system requirements?

Python 3.9+, macOS (Intel or Apple Silicon), Windows, or Linux. ~1GB disk space for AI models (downloaded once on first run). GPU is optional but speeds up stem separation significantly. 8GB RAM recommended.

What does reference mastering do?

Upload any professionally mastered track as a reference. RAWMASTER analyses its spectral profile (EQ curve), loudness, and dynamics — then matches your track to sound like it. This is what cloud mastering services charge monthly subscriptions for, but RAWMASTER runs locally and costs nothing after purchase.

Can I use the outputs commercially?

Yes. Your license, your outputs. Stems, remasters, and MIDI files are yours to use in released music, remixes, samples, or any commercial project.

What audio formats are supported?

MP3, WAV, FLAC, AIFF, OGG, and M4A. Output is always 24-bit WAV at 44.1kHz.
